Hello gang! After a week's rest from our last adventure to Prague, we're back in the EU again - this time to Paris, France! Join me as I recount our experiences in the City of Light.

## Day 1: Arrival
To get to Paris, I flew from Luton airport to Charles de Gaulle. The entire experience travelling from Cambridge to Luton, was an absolute chore - had to change transport modes multiple times, but surprisingly was able to make it in 2 hours flat! The flight then from Luton to CDG was uneventful, and we landed right on time. I happened to sit next to two British strangers who were also heading to Paris for the weekend. One of them was a bronze sculptor, who shared some fascinating stories about his work, and was showing off some of his pieces he was bringing to an exhibition in Paris. The girl was a bartender from Nottingham, and she was chatting about Radio One, some stell guy and a Red line? The sculptor seemed to know a lot about it, but I was lost lol.
The sculptor's shop site is [here](https://www.google.com/search?q=neil+carter+miniatures&num=10&client=ubuntu-sn&hs=aUe&sca_esv=b1dd116babca6fd0&channel=fs&sxsrf=AE3TifOo-rWwiXwLZx16U-OHHx2YEDqs4Q%3A1761539704253&ei=ePb-aLGND8X91e8PzNLJuQ0&ved=0ahUKEwixqdHixsOQAxXFfvUHHUxpMtcQ4dUDCBA&uact=5&oq=neil+carter+miniatures&gs_lp=Egxnd3Mtd2l6LXNlcnAiFm5laWwgY2FydGVyIG1pbmlhdHVyZXMyCBAAGIAEGLADMgkQABiwAxgIGB4yDhAAGIAEGLADGIYDGIoFMg4QABiABBiwAxiGAxiKBTILEAAYgAQYsAMYogQyCBAAGLADGO8FSKgCUABYAHABeACQAQCYAQCgAQCqAQC4AQPIAQCYAgGgAgyYAwCIBgGQBgaSBwExoAcAsgcAuAcAwgcDMi0xyAcH&sclient=gws-wiz-serp) btw!
But once I landed in CSG, the immigration line was the worst ever I've ever come across - took nearly an hour to get through! After that, I just headed to the RosiBus shuttle to get to the city center. At the shuttle stop, I met up this cute girl who solo travelling too! She was from Taiwan, and was exploring EU on her own! We chatted a bit while waiting for the bus, and I forgot to exchange contacts with her! But nevertheless, the bus ride into the city was super long due to the traffic. Lesson learned: Always take the metro from the airport next time!
I then took the metro to my AirBnb, which was located in the 11th arrondissement. The host was super friendly and tried to talk to me in French, which I appreciated and I tried to do the same in return. But my French is super rusty, so I mostly just pointed and spoke in very broken sentences. I bought a pain au chocolat from a nearby bakery, along with some Lays and Subway for dinner and then headed back to the apartment.
The apartment was cozy enough for the night and I quickly drifted off to sleep after a long day of travel.

## Day 2: Exploring Paris
Woke up early the next day, and took the metro direct to the Champs-Élysées. The weather was perfect - sunny with a light breeze. I strolled down the avenue, admiring the high-end shops and cafes. Since it was early in the morning, a lot of the street was still quiet, which made for a peaceful walk. I stopped by the Louis Vuitton store, just to window shop - very massive store by the way and made my way to the Arc de Triomphe. The monument was even more impressive in person. I however decided not to get close, as I wanted to save my energy for the other stuff I had planned for the day.
After a quick selfie, I had breakfast at a nearby café - a croissant, café au lait and orange jus. Delicious! From there, I headed to the Eiffel Tower. I did see a lot of homeless people around the area, which was a bit sad. Also while walking across Seine river, I saw the Musée du quai Branly - a building with a lot of forage plants growing on it - looked really pretty, but I didn't have the time to explore it :(.

The Eiffel Tower was everything I didn't expect and more. It was massive and awe-inspiring, as a reminder to the engineering marvels of the 19th century. I didn't go up the tower, nor the gardens around it - but definitely will have to tackle them next time I am in Paris. I then crossed the river again to get to the Place de Varsovie. From there, you can see the tower from an elevated-front angle, and it makes for a great photo shot! I didn't get the chance to explore the park around it, nor the Fountaine de Varsovie, but it looked really nice from afar.
I then decided to walk along Seine, to meet up with my friend at Notre Dame. The walk was really pleasant, with a lot of tourists, runners and occasional street vendors. I also did see a lot of things from afar - Hôtel des Invalides, Grand Palais, Théâtre du Rond-Point. Clicked a photo of the Eiffel tower from Pont Alexandre III - really picturesque spot!
Also saw the Place de la Concorde, Musée de l'Orangerie, and the Tuileries Garden from afar too :(. Didn't really have the time to explore them. Finally reached Notre Dame, which had been open to the public after the restoration from the fire a few years ago. Still, the exterior was beautiful. Unfortunately I did not manage to get inside, as the ticket queue was quite long. There was also the Sainte-Chapelle nearby which I did not manage to explore too :(.
I also met my friend there! He is a Parisian who I met during my years in Dublin (we were flatmates). We grabbed lunch at a nearby café - I had a chicken curry sandwich, café au lait and a canelé. After lunch, we decided to walk to the Palais Garnier while catching up on life. While walking, we passed by the Tour Saint-Jacques, a Gothic tower that was once part of a church. It served as a landmark for pilgrims on the Way of St. James.
The Palais Garnier was stunning from the inside - the grand staircase, the chandeliers, the ceiling paintings. But that was about it, there were no guided tours, very little description given the plaques, just made it feel like a photo-op spot. The Palais also contained a lot of halls which I assumed were for dining, balls and other events. Nothing I have not seen back in Delhi at Kota House every third Saturday lol.
After that, we then headed to the Sacre-Coeur in Montmartre. The walk up to the basilica was quite steep, unfortunately when we got to the top, we were greeted with a huge crowd of party tourists. Apparently there was some sort of music festival going on and the whole area was packed, with no moving space :'(. We decided to skip going inside the basilica and just took some photos from the foothill. From there, my friend took his leave, and I headed to the cloth shops he recommended nearby. Realising the market to be more suitable for women, I decided to just walk a bit and take the metro to the Panthéon. Once I reached Panthéon, I realised it was closed (5pm), so I just saw the beautiful architecture from outside. I then went to a nearby church - Église Saint-Étienne-du-Mont, which was open to the public. The interior was beautiful, with intricate stained glass windows and a peaceful atmosphere.
I then began heading back to the Airbnb, stopping for some a good dinner at a nearby Japanese resturant, Hot Pot Noodle Pantheon - had some ramen. Also on my way back to the apartment, I stepped into a supermarket (?) to grab some chocolate covered almonds and carrot juice. Parisians sure have a lot of variety when it comes to healthy food options.
And that wrapped up my second day in Paris!

## Day 3: Louvre and Chilling
For my third and final day I had decided to spend the majority time in Louvre museum, to see if it's worth the hype. Since I got tickets for 11:30am, I decided to have a breakfast and chill walk to the museum. Mornings in Paris are beautiful, as I walked through the empty streets of Paris, specifically, Place de la Bastille, Pont Saint-Louis, Ile Saint-Louis and the Point Neuf before reaching the museums. I would definitely recommend readers to watch [this video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DOH-HNotTaE) once to give you some backdrop on the history of these bridges and the isles.
I also had a hearty breakfast (omelettes, with 3 sides, and cafe au lait) at a nearby café followed by crepes from the Art Cafe, near Louvre Museum. I would suggest everyone should give them a try, they're really good!
I finally reached the Louvre Museum, and was greeted by the iconic glass pyramid entrance. The museum was massive, and I had only a few hours to explore it. I decided to focus on the highlights - while trying to look at everything. I started off with the
- Cour Marly, a spectacular covered court of the Louvre, formerly an open courtyard in the Richelieu wing, now roofed with glass to create a bright sculpture hall.
        * Exhibts 17-18th century French sculptures - most famous one being the pair known as Horses of Marly.
        * Some of the other notable sculptures include La Seine, Daphné, some plates which told the story of Paris, a lamp post and 4 statues representing the 4 seasons (I forgot to note down their names!)
- Egyptian Antiquities: with artifacts spanning from 4,000 BC to the 4th century AD. Some of the highlights include:
        * The Great Sphinx of Tanis: a massive granite sphinx that dates back to the 26th century BC.
        * The Seated Scribe: a lifelike statue of a scribe from the Old Kingdom period.
        * Various tomb artifacts, statues, and hieroglyphic inscriptions that provide insight into ancient Egyptian culture and beliefs.
- Islamic Art: spanning 13 centuries
- Basement floor: which has how Louvre was built, and some medieval remains of the fortress.
- The Mona Lisa was smaller than I expected, but still captivating.
- The Venus de Milo was impressive, with its graceful curves and missing arms adding to its mystique.
- Liberty leading the people: a powerful representation of the July Revolution of 1830 in France.
- The French Crown Jewels: a dazzling display of royal regalia, including crowns, scepters, and other precious items. And yes, this is before the [robbery of October 2025](https://abcnews.go.com/International/thieves-robbed-louvre-minutes-minute-minute-breakdown-daring/story?id=126719610)!
- The Christ detached from the cross

And a lot of stuff, which I completely forgot to note down. It is a really big museum, with quite a lot of description plaques (some of them in only French). I would recommend everyone to spend at least half a day here, if not a full day to really appreciate the art and history on display.
There is a saying that if you spend 10 seconds looking at each piece of art in the Louvre, it would take you 100 days to see everything. I can totally believe that now lol.
With this rushed visit spanning 4 hours, I decided to head soon for the airport. Grabbed myself a sandwich, *another* bakery item from a nearby café and took the metro to Charles de Gaulle. The metro ride was smooth, and I reached the airport with plenty of time to spare before my flight back to Luton. However the immigration line at CSG was again long, stressing out both us immigrants and British citizens alike. I would most definitely recommend everyone to consider other options than flying to Paris from Luton imo.
The journey back from Luton to Cambridge was quite tedious too - Luton has like three train stations, each having the name "Luton" in it, making it super confusing to navigate. I would say best bet in future is take the train to London, then to Cambridge, or take the National Express or Flexi Bus lol.

So that wraps up my Paris trip! Overall, it was a fantastic experience, and I can't wait to return and explore all the parts I missed out on :(! Definitely 2.5 days is not enough for this beautiful city. Until next time, au Renoir!
